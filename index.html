Our Approach

A straight shot to general intelligence.

Most labs split their attention between research and product, or crowd into the same handful of trendy approaches. We do neither. Millae is a pure-play research effort focused on fundamental questions — deliberately working the under-invested, unfashionable corners of AI research, largely outside large language models, where we believe the highest-leverage progress is still waiting to be found.

01

Fundamental research, not fashion

We work on the problems we think actually matter, not the ones currently in vogue. Much of our research lives outside large language models, in areas under-invested precisely because they aren't trendy.

02

Where we focus

Our research background is in reinforcement learning. Today we're working on mechanistic interpretability, meta-learning — especially meta-reinforcement learning — and drawing on insights from neuroscience and other disciplines outside machine learning.

03

Research-first, always

No product surface area, no distribution treadmill. A small team of researchers, unusually undistracted.

04

Built in Seoul

We're based in Seoul, South Korea — building a research culture from first principles, close to a deep pool of technical talent.

Why This Matters

The Great Project of our generation.

We believe developing human-level general intelligence is the great project of this generation, and of the generations after it — an undertaking on the scale of terraforming a new world. In Ada Palmer's Terra Ignota, the Utopian Hive commits itself for centuries to terraforming Mars, one small contribution at a time, as a shared goal larger than any single life. In Frank Herbert's Dune, the Fremen give generations to the slow work of transforming Arrakis. We're looking for scientists who think about their work the same way — not as a job, but as a life's work folded into something larger than themselves.

The Stakes

We take the scale of this project seriously in both directions. The potential benefits of human-level and superintelligent AI are extraordinary — and the risk it poses is the greatest existential threat humanity has ever faced. As the arrival of superintelligence comes into view, we also expect nations and organizations who see themselves falling behind to grow desperate about losing power, and some may turn to violent intervention rather than accept that outcome. We think both dangers deserve to be prepared for, not waved away, and we build with that in mind.

How We Work

We don't rush.

Good research takes time, and we're not interested in a publication treadmill to prove otherwise. We work at the pace the problem actually requires, and we'd rather build with the field than around it.

01

Slow science

We're philosophically aligned with Yoshua Bengio's call to rethink the pace of ML publication and the Slow Science manifesto. Science needs time to think, and time to fail. A farmer is one who waits, and who does not resist time.

02

Open by default

We believe the field goes further, in the long run, through open collaboration than through closed, siloed development. If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together. We intend to go far.
