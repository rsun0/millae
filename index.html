Our Mission

A straight shot to general intelligence.

Most companies split their attention between research and product, or crowd into the same handful of trendy approaches. We do neither. Millae is a pure-play research effort focused on fundamental questions — deliberately working the under-invested, unfashionable corners of AI research, largely outside large language models, where we believe the highest-leverage progress is still waiting to be found.

01

Fundamental research, not fashion

We work on the problems we think actually matter, not the ones currently in vogue. Much of our research lies outside large language models, in areas under-invested precisely because they aren't trendy.

02

Where we focus

Our research background is in reinforcement learning and LLMs. Today we're interested in creativity, long-term memory/compression, mechanistic interpretability, and drawing insights from other disciplines outside machine learning like psychology and neuroscience.

03

Research-first, always

No product surface area, no monetization. A small team of researchers, unusually undistracted.

Our Philosophy

We don't rush.

Good research takes time, and we're not interested in a publication treadmill to prove otherwise. We work at the pace the problem actually requires, and we'd rather walk in the right direction than risk sprinting in the wrong direction.

01

Slow science

We're philosophically aligned with Yoshua Bengio's call to rethink the pace of ML publication and the Slow Science manifesto. A tree that grows quickly may catch the eye, but it can be easily shaken when the wind comes. The more we rush to achieve, the more easily life is consumed; a life patiently built endures.

02

Committed to the open-source community

We believe the field goes further in the long run through open collaboration, rather than siloed proprietary efforts. We see ourselves as just one team contributing to the broader collective effort to reach general intelligence. If your lab is interested in collaborating with us, please email us.

"If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together."
